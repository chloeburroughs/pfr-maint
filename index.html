A few nights ago, we learned that so many Americans, too many, have not learned or grown or done better. They have sided with the self-interest of fascistic power. Today, we’re working behind the scenes to bring PFR back, and make it bigger. It’s not enough to make art and write poetry and prose. We must do those things, as they are fundamental reflections of our humanity, but we must work so much harder and go so much farther.

We’re bringing our entire back-catalog online soon, ad-free and paywall-free. But we’re also going to spend this next presidential term building mutual aid networks, enabling critical whistleblowers, spotlighting the damaging actions of those in power, and partnering with those that share these missions. We will persist. We will survive. We will, in the words of our queer activist for parents, ACT UP and FIGHT.

I urge you to join us, either behind the banner of PFR, or with another organization or communities with similar goals.
